Build Module 15: Final Report Generator for the existing EU AI Act Compliance Readiness Dashboard.

Flowchart section: none (practical add-on, not in the Future of Life flowchart). Legal basis: aggregates all — Article 2, 4, 5, 6/Annex I/III, 9-15, 17, 22-25, 26-27, 43/47-49, 50, 51/53/55, 72-73; Annex IV, Annex VIII; Recital 47, 166. Depends on: Modules 1-14.

Apply the shared conventions from Module 4. This module performs NO new classification — it reads the result objects of Modules 1-14, joins them on system_id, cites legal_basis_citation per obligation, and rolls per-system statuses, readiness scores and remediation progress into an organisation-level report. Its distinctive job is provenance and honesty: every obligation shows the article/annex it rests on, its applicable_from_date, and whether the rule derives from final law or from draft/provisional guidance (the May 2026 Digital Omnibus and the 2026-05-19 draft high-risk guidelines). Anything on non-final text is badged so the report never presents a provisional deadline or an in-flux obligation as settled law.

Routes: /report (organisation-level dashboard), /report/systems/[id] (per-system report page assembling every module's output for that system, with a badged obligations register, readiness score, remediation board, and a legal-basis footnote per obligation), and /report/non-final-guidance (the dedicated appendix of obligations resting on draft/provisional guidance).

Questionnaire (scoping only — the report consumes upstream answers):
1. Which systems should the report cover? Helper: default all inventoried systems; scope to a subset for a targeted board/auditor report. Options: All systems · High-risk only · By business unit · By role (provider/deployer) · Custom selection
2. How should obligations resting on draft/provisional guidance be presented? Helper: Conservative = include and badge as 'timeline/obligation not yet final'; Excluded = omit non-final rules (risk: under-stating future duties). Default conservative — include and badge, never silently drop. Options: Include and badge (recommended) · Include without badge · Exclude non-final rules · Not sure
3. Who is the audience? Helper: internal (full detail, all uncertainties), board/executive (summary + risk view), external auditor/regulator (evidence citations + legal basis emphasised). Does not change the underlying data. Options: Internal compliance · Board / executive · External auditor / regulator · Not sure
4. Include the full remediation plan or a progress summary only? Helper: full = every remediation item with owner/due date (Module 14); summary = progress percentages and overdue counts. Options: Full remediation plan · Progress summary only · Not sure

Deterministic rules (src/lib/report/reportRules.ts) — pure aggregation, no LLM reasoning, no new classification:
- aggregation_logic: for each system, pull status from Modules 3-11, the consolidated obligation set from Module 12, readiness from Module 13, remediation progress from Module 14; join on system_id. Roll up to organisation level: counts by risk tier, average readiness score, total open/overdue remediation items, count of obligations resting on non-final guidance.
- non_final_flagging_logic: any obligation line with guidance_status ∈ {draft, provisional} is badged and collected into a "Obligations resting on draft/provisional guidance" appendix, with its source named (Digital Omnibus 2026-05 provisional agreement, or draft high-risk guidelines 2026-05-19). A report is never allowed to present a provisional deadline or in-flux obligation as final. This is the module's headline behaviour.
- legal_basis_citation: carried through verbatim per obligation from the source module (Module 12 canonical). The report cites, it does not invent, legal basis.
- applicable_from_date: carried through, showing the split timeline explicitly — prohibited practices 2025-02-02 (final), AI literacy 2025-02-02 (final, possibly softened), GPAI 2025-08-02 (final), transparency 2026-08-02 (final), Annex III high-risk 2027-12-02 (provisional — Digital Omnibus deferral), Annex I high-risk 2028-08-02 (provisional).
- report_sections: (1) Executive summary — org-level readiness snapshot, counts by risk tier, headline risks, count of obligations on non-final guidance; (2) Per-system pages — role (M2), definition gate (M3), scope/exclusions (M4-5), prohibited (M6), high-risk (M7), literacy (M8), reclassification (M9), GPAI (M10), transparency/FRIA (M11), obligations matrix (M12), readiness (M13), remediation (M14); (3) Obligations register — every applicable obligation across all systems with legal_basis_citation, applicable_from_date, guidance_status, readiness and remediation status; (4) Draft/provisional-guidance appendix; (5) Cross-module consistency warnings; (6) Assumptions & disclaimers — date checked, guidance versions, standard non-legal-advice disclaimer.

Result object (organisation-level report + per-system pages, aggregation only):
- classification_or_status: org-level readiness using 'likely/possible/needs review' wording (e.g. "N systems assessed: A likely high-risk, B possible high-risk needs review, C not high-risk, D prohibited-practice flags"). Per-obligation lines carry the source module's status label. Never "compliant"/"legally confirmed" — a readiness snapshot, not an attestation.
- confidence_score (measures confidence in the aggregated picture, not compliance): coverage-weighted roll-up — minimum/weighted-average of upstream module confidences per system, minus penalties for systems with 'needs review' statuses or missing upstream modules. Standard labels.
- reasoning_summary, positive/negative indicators (negatives: systems missing upstream modules; provisional deadlines shown without the badge; upstream 'needs review' rolled into a confident headline; remediation progress reported as compliance), key_uncertainties (the split timeline itself is provisional; high-risk classification rests on draft guidelines; AI literacy may be softened; GPAI systemic-risk designations are Commission-discretionary), missing_fields, recommended_next_questions (exclude or run-first systems missing modules? regenerate once the Omnibus is finalised? which audience version? exclude 'needs review' systems from the headline figure?).
- role_conditional_obligation / reclassification_trigger_flags / registration_required / standards_conformity_route / not_high_risk_documentation_flag: all REPORTED, not recomputed — per system from Modules 2/9/12/13. A reclassified system shows the expanded provider obligation set with the trigger named; registration status (done/outstanding) and the non-public variant are listed; conformity route shown because it affects remediation-timeline realism (flag schedule-risk where harmonised standards are not yet published); a not-high-risk system with the Art 6(3) flag is shown as having the self-assessment + registration outstanding, so it is not reported as "nothing to do".

UI: summary cards (systems covered, likely high-risk, prohibited-practice flags, GPAI models, average readiness %, open/overdue remediation items, obligations on non-final guidance, overall report confidence); results table = portfolio obligations register, one row per system × obligation (system, risk tier, role, obligation, legal basis, applicable from, guidance status, readiness, remediation status, owner, due date, confidence, key uncertainty); CSV exports the full portfolio register with legal_basis_citation, applicable_from_date, guidance_status, readiness and remediation state — the single canonical export of the whole assessment; ALSO support PDF export of the formatted report (executive summary + per-system pages + non-final-guidance appendix); filters (system, risk tier, role, obligation, guidance status, readiness status, remediation status, applicable-from range, confidence). Cross-module consistency warning: aggregate and display every upstream consistency warning (e.g. "This system's obligation set rests on a Module 2 role still marked needs review; the reported obligations may change once the role is confirmed").
