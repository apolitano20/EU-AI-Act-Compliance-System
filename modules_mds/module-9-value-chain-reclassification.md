Build Module 9: Value-Chain Reclassification for the existing EU AI Act Compliance Readiness Dashboard.

Flowchart section: Article 25 provider-conversion risk (referenced but not fully modelled in Module 2). Legal basis: Article 25. Depends on: Module 1, Module 2, Module 7 (high-risk status). Feeds: Module 12 (obligations matrix).

Apply the shared conventions from Module 4. Module 2 already collects the raw signal (Article25ProviderConversionRisk flag); this module converts it into an explicit reclassification determination. It is the AUTHORITATIVE writer of reclassification_trigger_flags consumed downstream.

Article 25 reallocates responsibility along the value chain: a distributor, importer, deployer, or other third party becomes a "provider" of a high-risk system — inheriting the full Article 16 provider obligation set — in three cases: (a) they put their own name/trademark on a high-risk system already on the market; (b) they make a substantial modification to a high-risk system already placed on the market/put into service such that it remains high-risk; or (c) they modify the intended purpose of a system (including a GPAI system) not previously high-risk such that it becomes high-risk.

Routes: /reclassification (list) and /reclassification/systems/[id] (detail — each trigger question/answer, fired VCR rules with Article 25 citations, promoted role_conditional_obligation set, original-provider cooperation duty, needs-review banner where reliant on draft high-risk guidelines).

Questionnaire (reuse-first):
1. Have you placed your own name or trademark on an AI system originally provided by someone else? Helper: rebranding/white-labelling makes you its provider for AI Act purposes even if you did not build it. (module-2 Article25ProviderConversionRisk) Options: Yes · No · Not sure
2. Have you made a substantial modification to a high-risk AI system after it was placed on the market/put into service? Helper: a 'substantial modification' (Art 3(23)) is a change not foreseen in the original conformity assessment that either affects compliance with the high-risk requirements or changes intended purpose; a pre-planned, already-assessed change is NOT substantial; routine in-scope retraining generally does not qualify. (module-2 flag; module-7 status) Options: Yes · No · Not sure · Not applicable (not high-risk)
3. Have you changed the intended purpose of an AI system (including a GPAI system) in a way that turns it into a high-risk system? Helper: e.g. taking a general-purpose model and deploying it to score creditworthiness or filter job applicants. (module-2 flag; module-7 use-case mapping) Options: Yes · No · Not sure
4. What is your current role for this system before any reclassification? Helper: only deployers, distributors, importers or other third parties can be converted into providers; if you are already the original provider this module does not apply. (module-2 role) Options: Deployer · Distributor · Importer · Other third party · Already the provider · Not sure

Deterministic rules (src/lib/reclassification/reclassificationRules.ts):
- VCR-1 rebrand — role IN {deployer, distributor, importer, third_party} AND own_name_or_trademark AND high-risk → reclassify to provider; set rebranded=true. [Art 25(1)(a)] final. applicable_from_date 2026-08-02 (note the Annex III → 2027-12-02 and Annex I → 2028-08-02 Digital Omnibus timeline split).
- VCR-2 substantial-modification — role IN {…} AND substantial_modification AND remains high-risk → reclassify to provider; apply the two-prong Art 3(23) test; set substantial_modification=true. [Art 25(1)(b); Art 3(23)] final.
- VCR-3 purpose-change-to-high-risk — role IN {…} AND intended_purpose_changed AND resulting system now high-risk (module-7 re-evaluation) → reclassify to provider; applies even to GPAI systems repurposed into a high-risk use; set purpose_changed_to_high_risk=true. [Art 25(1)(c)] final.
- VCR-4 original-provider-cooperation — when any of VCR-1/2/3 fires, the original provider ceases to be provider of that system BUT remains bound by Art 25(2)-(3) cooperation duties (supply technical documentation/information + reasonable access). Emit an informational obligation row for the original provider. [Art 25(2), 25(3)] final.
- VCR-5 draft-guidelines caveat — the precise boundary of 'substantial modification' and 'becomes high-risk' depends partly on the 2026-05-19 draft Commission high-risk classification guidelines (not final); any borderline determination relying on them carries guidance_status: draft and is surfaced as needs-review, not confirmed.

Result object:
- classification_or_status: "Provider reclassification likely triggered — full provider obligation set now applies" (needs_review where reliant on draft guidelines); or "no reclassification".
- confidence_score baseline 75 (medium) — the operative predicates ('substantial modification', 'becomes high-risk') are judgement-laden and partly rest on non-final guidelines, and the underlying Module 7 high-risk status must be settled before triggers (b)/(c) can be confirmed.
- reasoning_summary, positive/negative indicators, key_uncertainties, missing_fields (module-7 high-risk outcome; detail of the modification/purpose change — only signalled by the module-2 flag), recommended_next_questions.
- role_conditional_obligation: deployer/distributor/importer → on any trigger, promoted to provider with the full Article 16 set (risk management, data governance, technical documentation, logging, transparency to deployers, human oversight, accuracy/robustness/cybersecurity, QMS, conformity assessment, registration); original provider → released as provider of the reclassified system but retains Art 25(2)-(3) cooperation duties.
- reclassification_trigger_flags: THIS module writes them (defaults false, overwritten by fired VCR rules). Any true value promotes the actor to provider and re-invokes Module 12 under the provider role.
- registration_required: conditional — a reclassified provider of a high-risk system inherits Art 49/Annex VIII registration (non-public variant for law-enforcement/migration/border).
- standards_conformity_route: conditional — reclassified provider must complete conformity assessment (harmonised-standards presumption where available, else notified body / internal control per Art 43).
- not_high_risk_documentation_flag: conditional — where a purpose change is assessed and the result is NOT high-risk, retain and register the Article 6(3) self-assessment; "no reclassification" ≠ "nothing to document".
