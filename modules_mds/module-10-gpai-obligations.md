Build Module 10: GPAI Obligations for the existing EU AI Act Compliance Readiness Dashboard.

Flowchart section: #R1. Legal basis: Article 51, 53, 55, 25; Annex XI, XII, XIII; Recital 97, 110. Depends on: Module 1. Feeds: Module 9 (fine-tuning reclassification), Module 12.

Apply the shared conventions from Module 4. Reuse-first: reuse Module 1 (usesGpaiOrLlm, underlyingModelOrTool, modelProvider, buildType, fineTunedOrRetrainedModel) first; ask only what is missing.

Covers three angles: (1) the organisation is itself a provider of a GPAI model (Article 53, and Article 55 if systemic-risk); (2) the reciprocal downstream case — the organisation builds on a third-party GPAI model and relies on Article 25/53 information-flow; (3) a GPAI model integrated into a high-risk system. Systemic-risk is NOT purely the 10^25 FLOP presumption — the Commission can also designate on capability/user-base/scalability/autonomy grounds (Art 51(1)(b), Annex XIII), so surface it as an UNCERTAINTY, not a hard rule.

Routes: /gpai (list) and /gpai/systems/[id] (detail — inventory evidence used, result panel, indicators, uncertainties, missing fields, next questions, applicable dates per obligation, link back to inventory).

Questionnaire (plain English):
- gpai_q1 Does your organisation develop, train, or place on the market a general-purpose AI model (broadly capable — LLM, foundation, multimodal)? Helper: the model itself, not a narrow single-purpose tool. (module-1 usesGpaiOrLlm, underlyingModelOrTool, buildType) Options: Yes · No · Not sure · Not applicable
- gpai_q2 Was this model trained using a very large amount of computing power? Helper: presumed systemic risk when cumulative training compute > 10^25 FLOP; if unknown, answer Not sure — provider/vendor docs may state it. Options: Yes, above 10^25 FLOP · No, below 10^25 FLOP · Not sure · Not applicable
- gpai_q3 Has the Commission (or AI Office) designated your model as GPAI with systemic risk, or notified you that it may be? Helper: designation can rest on evaluated capabilities, user base, market reach, scalability, or tool access/autonomy (Art 51(1)(b), Annex XIII) — not a fixed number. Options: Yes · No · Not sure · Not applicable
- gpai_q4 Is your GPAI model released under a free and open-source licence with parameters, architecture and usage info publicly available? Helper: open-source GPAI gets a partial exemption from some Art 53 documentation duties, but NOT if systemic risk; copyright policy and training-content summary duties still apply. Options: Yes · No · Not sure · Not applicable
- gpai_q5 Does this system build on, call, or fine-tune a GPAI model provided by another company (external LLM API or downloaded foundation model)? Helper: the downstream/reciprocal case — you rely on the upstream provider giving you Annex XII information. (module-1 usesGpaiOrLlm, modelProvider, buildType) Options: Yes · No · Not sure · Not applicable
- gpai_q6 Have you fine-tuned, retrained, or substantially modified a third-party GPAI model? Helper: can make YOU a provider of the modified model (Art 53, and Art 55 if your training compute crosses the threshold); links to Module 9. (module-1 fineTunedOrRetrainedModel; module-2 Art 25 conversion risk) Options: Yes · No · Not sure · Not applicable
- gpai_q7 Is a GPAI model integrated into a system that could be high-risk (recruitment, credit, biometrics, critical infrastructure)? Helper: the downstream provider needs sufficient information/cooperation from the model provider; full high-risk classification is Module 7 — here we flag the dependency. (module-1 riskDomainFlags, affectsDecisionsAboutPeople) Options: Yes · No · Not sure · Not applicable
- gpai_q8 If you provide a GPAI model, have you put in place an EU copyright-compliance policy and a sufficiently detailed public summary of the training content? Helper: Art 53(1)(c)/(d) apply to ALL GPAI providers, including open-source. Options: Yes · No · Partly · Not sure · Not applicable

Deterministic rules (src/lib/gpai/rules.ts):
- GPAI-R1 Provider of a GPAI model — gpai_q1=Yes → flag Article 53 duties: technical documentation (Annex XI), downstream information (Annex XII), EU copyright policy, public training-content summary. [Art 53; Annex XI, XII; Recital 97]
- GPAI-R2 Systemic-risk presumption (compute) — gpai_q2=Yes (>10^25 FLOP) → presumed systemic risk; add Article 55 on top of Art 53; provider must notify the Commission within 2 weeks of meeting/expecting to meet the threshold. [Art 51(1)(a), 51(2); Art 52; Art 55]
- GPAI-R3 Systemic-risk designation (qualitative) — gpai_q3=Yes OR model among the most advanced/very high reach → POSSIBLE systemic risk even below the FLOP threshold. Surface as UNCERTAINTY, not a hard rule (Commission decision under Annex XIII). [Art 51(1)(b); Annex XIII; Recital 110]
- GPAI-R4 Article 55 systemic-risk obligations — GPAI-R2 OR GPAI-R3 → flag model evaluation + adversarial testing (red-teaming), systemic-risk assessment & mitigation, serious-incident tracking/reporting to the AI Office, adequate cybersecurity. [Art 55(1)(a)-(d)]
- GPAI-R5 Open-source partial exemption — gpai_q4=Yes AND NOT (GPAI-R2 OR GPAI-R3) → exempt from Annex XI technical docs and Annex XII downstream info, but copyright policy and training-content summary still apply; exemption VOID if systemic risk. [Art 53(2); Art 53(1)(c)-(d); Recital 104]
- GPAI-R6 Downstream provider — information-flow reliance — gpai_q5=Yes → downstream provider/deployer; flag the right to receive Annex XII information and cooperation from the upstream provider; capture whether it was actually received (feeds Module 12 as a supply-chain evidence item). [Art 53(1)(b); Annex XII; Art 25(4)]
- GPAI-R7 Fine-tuning/substantial modification converts to provider — gpai_q6=Yes → may become provider of the modified model, inheriting Art 53 (scoped to the change) and Art 55 if the modification compute crosses the threshold; set a reclassification trigger for Module 9. [Art 25(1); Recital 109; Art 53]
- GPAI-R8 GPAI integrated into a high-risk system — gpai_q7=Yes → flag cross-dependency (the high-risk downstream provider needs information/cooperation from the model provider); defer full high-risk classification to Module 7; raise a cross-module note. [Art 25(4); Art 53(1)(b); Annex XII]
- GPAI-R9 Copyright policy and training-content summary — gpai_q1=Yes (any GPAI provider incl. open-source) → flag mandatory EU copyright policy + public training-content summary (AI Office template). [Art 53(1)(c); 53(1)(d)]

Result object:
- classification_or_status: likely_gpai_provider | possibly_systemic_risk_needs_review | downstream_consumer | gpai_in_high_risk_context | likely_not_gpai | insufficient_information. Never "compliant".
- confidence_score: start 100; −15 per core 'Not sure' (is-a-GPAI-model / training-compute / model-provider); −25 if systemic-risk status unknown but model is large; −20 if downstream information receipt unknown; −10 per missing essential field (cap −40); −15 for contradictions. Standard labels.
- reasoning_summary, positive/negative indicators, key_uncertainties (systemic-risk designation beyond FLOP is Commission-discretionary; whether fine-tuning crosses the provider threshold; whether Annex XII info was actually received; FLOP often undisclosed), missing_fields (training_compute_flop, gpai_provider_name, systemic_risk_designation_status, annex_xii_information_received, fine_tuning_compute), recommended_next_questions.
- role_conditional_obligation: GPAI_model_provider → Art 53 (+ Art 55 if systemic; + Art 54 EU authorised rep if established outside the EU); downstream_provider_deployer → rely on Annex XII, no Art 53 model-provider duties unless converted by modification; modifier_fine_tuner → may inherit Art 53 (scoped) via Art 25, re-evaluate on Module 9.
- registration_required: GPAI providers do NOT register in the Annex VIII high-risk database, but systemic-risk providers must NOTIFY the Commission (Art 52) within 2 weeks; if the GPAI is embedded in a high-risk system, Art 49/Annex VIII applies at the system level.
- not_high_risk_documentation_flag: a downstream provider building on a GPAI model should still document why the resulting system is not high-risk (Art 6(3)); "GPAI-only" ≠ "nothing to do".
