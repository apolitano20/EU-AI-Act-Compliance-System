Build Module 14: Remediation Tracker for the existing EU AI Act Compliance Readiness Dashboard.

Flowchart section: none (practical add-on, not in the Future of Life flowchart). Legal basis: Article 9-15 (gaps inherited via Module 13/12), 17, 26, 43, 47-49, 72, 73; Annex IV, Annex VIII. Depends on: Module 13. Feeds: Module 15.

Apply the shared conventions from Module 4. This module turns each Module 13 evidence gap (readiness status 'partial evidence', 'evidence gap', 'not started' or 'unknown') into a trackable remediation action item with an owner, a due date and a status. It does NOT re-derive applicability (Module 12) or evidence state (Module 13) — it manages closing gaps already identified.

Critical distinction: separate one-off tasks (e.g. author the Annex IV file) from recurring/lifecycle controls (post-market monitoring Art 72, serious-incident reporting readiness Art 73, periodic QMS review Art 17, continuous risk management Art 9) that must be re-verified on a cadence and can never be permanently "done". Due dates are anchored to the per-obligation applicable_from_date carried through from Module 12/13 so the tracker reflects the real (provisional) enforcement timeline, not an arbitrary date. Completing a recurring item schedules its next occurrence rather than closing it.

Routes: /remediation (list, one row per remediation item) and /remediation/systems/[id] (detail — per-system Kanban board by status plus a recurring-controls calendar; each item shows source gap, required artifacts from Module 13, owner, due date, priority, legal basis + deadline badge, and for recurring items the cadence and next-due date).

Questionnaire: one planning question per Module 13 gap row, plus recurrence-configuration for lifecycle obligations. Seeded from Module 13; only shown for obligations whose readiness_status is not 'evidence in place' or 'not applicable'.
1. Who is the owner responsible for closing this gap? Helper: named person/role (Head of ML, DPO, Quality Manager); an owner is required before an item moves past 'open'. Options: <free text owner> · Unassigned · Not sure
2. What is the target due date? Helper: default derived from the obligation's applicable_from_date minus a lead-time buffer; for high-risk rows this is the provisional 2027-12-02 (Annex III) or 2028-08-02 (Annex I); override with your internal deadline. Options: <date> · Use suggested date from applicable_from_date · Not sure
3. Is this a one-off task or a recurring control? Helper: one-off = produce an artifact once (write Annex IV file, sign declaration of conformity); recurring = a control re-verified on a cadence, never permanently complete (Art 72, 73, 17, 9). Options: One-off task · Recurring control · Not sure
4. (recurring only) How often must it be reviewed? Options: Monthly · Quarterly · Semi-annually · Annually · Event-driven (re-arm on trigger) · Custom · Not sure
5. What is the current status? Helper: Open → In progress → Blocked → Completed (one-off) or → Verified/Re-armed (recurring). 'Completed' on a recurring item schedules the next occurrence. Options: Open · In progress · Blocked · Completed · Verified · Deferred · Not applicable
6. What is the priority? Helper: computed from nearness of applicable_from_date and gap size ('not started' > 'partial'); prohibited-practice and already-in-force items (AI literacy, transparency) outrank deferred high-risk items; override allowed. Options: Critical · High · Medium · Low · Not sure

Deterministic rules (src/lib/remediation/remediationRules.ts): for each Module 13 gap, generate a remediation item — suggested due date from applicable_from_date, one-off-vs-recurring from a static obligation_id → recurrence map, priority from (deadline proximity × gap size × guidance_status). No LLM reasoning. Inherit legal_basis_citation, applicable_from_date, guidance_status per obligation. Recurring items are re-armed on completion, not closed.
- recurrence_map: OBL-ART72-PMM recurring; OBL-ART73-INCIDENT recurring/event-driven; OBL-ART17-QMS recurring; OBL-ART9-RMS recurring; OBL-ART10-DATA one-off + review-on-change; OBL-ART11-TECHDOC one-off + keep-current; OBL-ART12-LOGGING one-off design + recurring retention checks; OBL-ART13-TRANSP-DEPLOYER one-off + update-on-change; OBL-ART14-OVERSIGHT one-off design + recurring operator training; OBL-ART15-ACCURACY one-off + re-test-on-change; OBL-ART26-DEPLOYER one-off setup + recurring monitoring/logging; OBL-ART43-CONFORMITY one-off (re-run on substantial modification); OBL-ART49-REGISTER one-off + update-on-change; OBL-ART6-3-NOTHR-DOC one-off + review-on-change.
- due_date_logic: suggested_due_date = obligation.applicable_from_date − lead_time_buffer, where the buffer is larger for OBL-ART43-CONFORMITY on the notified-body route (long lead times). Already-in-force obligations (AI literacy 2025-02-02, GPAI 2025-08-02, transparency 2026-08-02) get an immediate/near-term due date, not the deferred high-risk date.
- priority_logic: priority = f(deadline_proximity, gap_size, guidance_status); nearer date → higher; 'not started' > 'partial'; in-force obligations outrank deferred high-risk ones; draft/provisional items are surfaced but flagged 'timeline may shift' so effort is not mis-prioritised against a date that could move.
- Example generated items: REM-ART11-TECHDOC (author + maintain Annex IV file; one-off + keep-current; provisional; High); REM-ART72-PMM (stand up + run post-market monitoring; recurring monthly review; provisional; Medium); REM-ART73-INCIDENT (establish incident-reporting procedure + owner; recurring/event-driven; provisional; Medium); REM-ART4-LITERACY (deliver AI-literacy measures; recurring annual refresh; already in force — immediate; Art 4; High).

Result object:
- classification_or_status: per-item status (open / in progress / blocked / completed / verified / deferred / not applicable) plus a system-level "X of Y gaps have an owner and due date, Z closed, N recurring controls active" summary. Factual progress wording only — a closed item means a gap was addressed, NOT that the system is legally compliant.
- confidence_score (measures completeness of the remediation plan, not compliance): start 100; −15 per gap with no owner, −15 per gap with no due date, −20 if the parent Module 13 readiness picture is low-confidence, −10 per recurring control with no cadence set. Standard labels.
- reasoning_summary, positive/negative indicators (negatives: unassigned gaps; recurring controls tracked as one-off and marked permanently done; due dates past the provisional applicable_from_date with no lead-time buffer; plan built on a low-confidence Module 13), key_uncertainties (provisional deadlines; conformity route; AI-literacy softening; a Module 9 reclassification adding new obligations after the plan was built), missing_fields, recommended_next_questions.
- role_conditional_obligation / reclassification_trigger_flags / registration_required / standards_conformity_route / not_high_risk_documentation_flag: all inherited from Module 12/13. If Module 9 reclassifies a deployer/distributor to provider, the plan is stale — regenerate to add provider-obligation remediation items; preserve owners/due dates already set on shared items. Flag the conformity item as schedule-risk when harmonised standards are not yet published (the due date may be undeliverable regardless of owner effort).

UI: summary cards (total items, open, in progress, blocked, completed, unassigned, overdue/due within 30 days, recurring controls active, overall remediation progress %); table one row per item (system, task, source obligation, legal basis, applicable from, guidance status, owner, due date, recurrence, status, priority, confidence, key uncertainty, actions); CSV exports items with owner/due_date/recurrence/status/source_obligation/legal_basis_citation/applicable_from_date/guidance_status for Module 15; filters (status, owner, priority, recurrence, obligation, guidance status, due-date range overdue/30d/90d, system). Cross-module consistency warning: flag when the plan was built from a low-confidence/'needs review' Module 13 picture, or when a Module 9 reclassification has fired since the plan was built (regenerate to capture additional gaps).

Implementation: rules in src/lib/remediation/remediationRules.ts; helpers generateRemediationItems(system) (from Module 13 gaps), suggestDueDate(obligation), classifyRecurrence(obligation_id), computePriority(item), completeItem(item) (re-arms recurring items). Self-check tests: technical-documentation gap → REM-ART11 one-off, due before high-risk date; post-market-monitoring gap → recurring item, completing it schedules the next occurrence (does not close); unassigned gap → confidence reduced, cannot pass 'open'; in-force AI-literacy gap prioritised above a deferred high-risk gap.

Acceptance criteria: /remediation list + detail; reads Module 13 gaps without recomputing them; one-off vs recurring correctly classified; recurring items re-arm on completion; due dates anchored to (provisional) applicable_from_date with a lead-time buffer; in-force obligations prioritised over deferred high-risk ones; Module 9 reclassification marks the plan stale; disclaimer footer; CSV export; tests cover recurrence, due-date and priority logic.
