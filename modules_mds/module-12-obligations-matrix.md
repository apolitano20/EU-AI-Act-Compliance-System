Build Module 12: Obligations Matrix for the existing EU AI Act Compliance Readiness Dashboard.

Flowchart section: "Understanding your obligations". Legal basis: Article 9-15, 17, 22-24, 26, 43, 47-49, 72-73; Recital 47, 166. Depends on: Modules 2, 4, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, 11. Feeds: Module 13, Module 15.

Apply the shared conventions from Module 4. This module performs NO new classification — it aggregates upstream flags into a consolidated per-system, per-role obligations checklist. Every applicable article is decomposed into a concrete obligation ROW (not left as an article-level reference), switched on/off deterministically by upstream flags. No LLM reasoning.

Aggregation logic: row.emitted = trigger(upstreamFlags) === true. Role determines which duty-holder column is populated (provider/deployer/importer/distributor/auth-rep). A Module 9 reclassification flag re-runs the provider trigger set against a deployer/distributor. A prohibited flag (Module 6) short-circuits the matrix to a single "must not place on market" row. Out-of-scope (Module 4) or "likely not an AI system" (Module 3) suppresses obligation rows and raises a cross-module consistency warning instead.

Routes: /obligations (list, one row per system, expandable to per-obligation rows) and /obligations/systems/[id] (detail — per-system checklist grouped by duty-holder role; each obligation shows legal basis, applicable date with provisional/draft badge, obligation type one-off vs recurring, and a link into Module 13).

Questionnaire (reuse M2-M11 first; ask only what upstream could not settle):
1. Should obligations be assessed for a specific entity role, or every role your organisation holds? (module-2 likelyRoles/possibleRoles) Options: Provider · Deployer · Importer · Distributor · Authorised representative · All roles held · Not sure
2. Has the system, its intended purpose, or its branding changed since first placed on the market/put into service? Helper: substantial modification, re-branding, or a purpose change to high-risk can convert a deployer/distributor into a provider (Art 25); Module 9 screens this. Options: Yes · No · Not sure · Not applicable
3. For a system not classified high-risk, has the provider documented the "no significant risk" self-assessment? Helper: Art 6(3) lets a provider treat an Annex III system as not-high-risk only if documented and (Art 49) registered. (module-7 derogation) Options: Yes, documented · No · Not sure · Not applicable (system is high-risk)
4. Is your organisation established outside the EU while placing this system on the EU market/putting it into service? Helper: a non-EU provider of a high-risk system or GPAI model must appoint an EU authorised representative before market placement (Art 22 / Art 54). (module-4 scope; module-2) Options: Yes · No · Not sure · Not applicable
5. For a high-risk system, do published harmonised standards cover its intended purpose, or will a notified body be involved? Helper: harmonised standards → self-assess under presumption of conformity (Annex VI); biometric/some Annex III cases or absence of standards → notified body (Annex VII). (module-7 Annex I vs III) Options: Harmonised standards apply · Notified body required · No standards published yet · Not sure · Not applicable

Deterministic rules (src/lib/obligations/obligationRules.ts) — one rule per obligation row; a row is emitted only when its trigger evaluates true against upstream flags. Each row carries its own legal_basis_citation, applicable_from_date, guidance_status, source_version_date. Obligation TEXT is final law; where only the enforcement date rests on the unadopted Digital Omnibus, mark guidance_status: provisional; where the trigger depends on the 2026-05-19 high-risk guidelines, mark draft.

Result object:
- classification_or_status: per-row "likely applies / possibly applies / needs review / does not appear to apply", plus an overall "obligations identified: N likely / M needs review" summary. Never "compliant" or "legally confirmed".
- confidence_score: start 100; −20 if upstream high-risk classification is 'needs review', −15 per unresolved Module 2 role ambiguity, −15 if a Module 9 reclassification flag is unresolved, −10 per 'Not sure' matrix-specific answer, −20 if Module 4 scope unresolved. Standard labels.
- reasoning_summary, positive/negative indicators, key_uncertainties (high-risk enforcement dates rest on the unadopted Digital Omnibus; high-risk boundaries depend on draft guidelines; systemic-risk GPAI designation; whether AI literacy stays firm), missing_fields, recommended_next_questions.
- role_conditional_obligation: obligation set keyed to Module 2 role, re-evaluated on a Module 9 reclassification. provider → Art 9,10,11,12,13,14,15,17,43,47,48,49,72,73; deployer → Art 26, Art 27 (FRIA where triggered), Art 50 relevant transparency, Art 49(3) registration where public authority; importer → Art 23; distributor → Art 24; authorised representative → Art 22.
- reclassification_trigger_flags (from Module 9/2), registration_required (high_risk_annex_iii + article_6_3_not_high_risk_assessment), standards_conformity_route (harmonised-standards presumption vs notified body; many standards not yet published), not_high_risk_documentation_flag (set when Module 7 matched Annex III but Art 6(3) derogation claimed).

Cross-module consistency warning: flag when an upstream 'needs review'/uncertain status (Module 7 high-risk, Module 3 gate, Module 9 reclassification) undermines the obligation set.
