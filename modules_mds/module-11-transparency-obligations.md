Build Module 11: Transparency Obligations for the existing EU AI Act Compliance Readiness Dashboard.

Flowchart section: #R4, #R5. Legal basis: Article 50, Article 27 (FRIA), Annex III; Recital 96, 132-135. Depends on: Module 2 (role), Module 7 (high-risk status). Feeds: Module 12.

Apply the shared conventions from Module 4. Reuse-first: reuse Module 1 (interactsDirectlyWithPeople, generatesContent, outputTypes, systemTypes) and Module 2 (provider vs deployer role) first. Keep the Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment (FRIA, Article 27) as a DISTINCT sub-section — not merged into general transparency — because it has its own trigger set (public bodies, private providers of public services, and deployers running Annex III creditworthiness/credit-scoring or life/health-insurance risk assessment).

Routes: /transparency (list) and /transparency/systems/[id] (detail — a dedicated Article 50 result panel AND a separate FRIA result panel, indicators, uncertainties, missing fields, next questions, applicable dates per obligation).

Questionnaire (plain English):
- tr_q1 Does this system talk to or interact directly with people (chatbot, virtual assistant, voice bot)? Helper: they generally must be told they are dealing with AI — unless obvious from context. (module-1 interactsDirectlyWithPeople; systemTypes Chatbot) Options: Yes · No · Not sure · Not applicable
- tr_q2 Does the system generate or manipulate synthetic content — text, images, audio, video? Helper: output generally must be marked machine-readable as artificially generated/manipulated (watermark/metadata). (module-1 generatesContent; outputTypes) Options: Yes · No · Not sure · Not applicable
- tr_q3 Does the system produce image/audio/video that resembles real people/objects/places/events and could falsely appear authentic (a deepfake)? Helper: deployers must disclose artificial generation/manipulation; limited artistic/creative exceptions still require appropriate disclosure. Options: Yes · No · Not sure · Not applicable
- tr_q4 Is AI-generated or AI-assisted text published to inform the public about matters of public interest? Helper: must be disclosed as artificially generated UNLESS it underwent human review/editorial control with a person/organisation holding editorial responsibility. Options: Yes · No · Not sure · Not applicable
- tr_q5 Does the system recognise/infer people's emotions, or categorise people based on biometric data? Helper: deployers must inform exposed persons; note some workplace/education emotion-recognition uses are prohibited (Module 6). (module-1 systemTypes Biometric; profilesIndividuals) Options: Yes · No · Not sure · Not applicable
- tr_q6 For each trigger above, is your organisation the provider (build/place on market) or the deployer (use under your own authority)? Helper: direct-interaction (50(1)) and synthetic-content marking (50(2)) are PROVIDER duties; deepfake, public-interest text, and emotion/biometric disclosure (50(3)-(4)) are DEPLOYER duties; the same system can carry both. (module-2 role) Options: Provider · Deployer · Both · Not sure · Not applicable
- tr_q7 (FRIA) Is your organisation a public body, or a private organisation providing public services (healthcare, education, housing, social security, essential utilities)? Helper: such deployers of an Annex III high-risk system generally must complete a FRIA before first use. (module-2 / module-1 organisation type, deployment context) Options: Public body · Private provider of public services · Neither · Not sure · Not applicable
- tr_q8 (FRIA) Does your organisation use an AI system to evaluate creditworthiness/credit scoring, or to assess risk & pricing for life/health insurance? Helper: these two Annex III use cases (points 5(b)/5(c)) trigger a FRIA even for ordinary private deployers. (module-1 riskDomainFlags, business function) Options: Yes – creditworthiness/credit scoring · Yes – life/health insurance risk · No · Not sure · Not applicable

Deterministic rules (src/lib/transparency/rules.ts) — keep FRIA rules (FRIA-R*) structurally separate from Article 50 rules (TR-R*):
- TR-R1 Direct-interaction disclosure — tr_q1=Yes AND role=Provider AND not obvious to user → provider must inform users they are interacting with AI unless obvious from context. [Art 50(1); Recital 132]
- TR-R2 Synthetic-content marking — tr_q2=Yes AND role=Provider → mark outputs machine-readable as artificially generated/manipulated (robust, interoperable). Legacy systems on the market before 2026-08-02 get a grace period to 2026-12-02. [Art 50(2); Recital 133]
- TR-R3 Emotion-recognition / biometric-categorisation disclosure — tr_q5=Yes AND role=Deployer AND not prohibited under Art 5 → inform exposed persons; process personal data per GDPR; cross-check Module 6. [Art 50(3); Recital 132]
- TR-R4 Deepfake disclosure — tr_q3=Yes AND role=Deployer → disclose artificial generation/manipulation; reduced/adapted disclosure for evidently artistic/creative/satirical/fictional work, still required appropriately. [Art 50(4) first subpara; Recital 134]
- TR-R5 AI-generated public-interest text disclosure — tr_q4=Yes AND role=Deployer AND NOT (human editorial review + editorial responsibility) → disclose artificial generation. [Art 50(4) second subpara; Recital 135]
- TR-R6 Transparency exemption — law enforcement — system authorised by law to detect/prevent/investigate criminal offences → certain Art 50 duties relaxed subject to safeguards; flag for legal review, do not auto-clear. [Art 50 exemptions]
- FRIA-R1 FRIA — public body / public-service deployer — tr_q7 = Public body OR Private provider of public services AND the system is an Annex III high-risk system deployed by the organisation → FRIA required before first use (describe deployer processes, period/frequency of use, categories of affected persons, specific risks of harm, human-oversight measures, mitigation/governance); notify the market surveillance authority. [Art 27(1)-(3); Recital 96]
- FRIA-R2 FRIA — creditworthiness / insurance deployer — tr_q8=Yes (Annex III 5(b) or 5(c)) → FRIA required even for ordinary private deployers; same FRIA content set as FRIA-R1. [Art 27(1); Annex III 5(b), 5(c)]

Result object:
- classification_or_status: likely_transparency_duties | possibly_transparency_duties | needs_review | likely_no_transparency_duties; plus a distinct FRIA status (likely_required | possibly_required | not_required | needs_review). Never "compliant".
- confidence_score: start 100; −15 per core 'Not sure' (interacts / generates content / biometric / role); −20 if provider/deployer role unknown (drives which duty applies); −20 if organisation type unknown for FRIA; −10 per missing essential field (cap −40); −15 for contradictions. Standard labels.
- reasoning_summary, positive/negative indicators, key_uncertainties ('obvious from context'; artistic/editorial exceptions; whether the Annex III high-risk threshold FRIA depends on is met — resolved in Module 7; FRIA date follows the deferred Annex III timeline), missing_fields, recommended_next_questions.
- role_conditional_obligation: provider → Art 50(1) direct-interaction design + Art 50(2) synthetic-content marking (no FRIA); deployer → Art 50(3) emotion/biometric + Art 50(4) deepfake/public-interest text + Art 27 FRIA where triggered; both → duties accumulate.
- registration_required: Article 50 alone does not trigger Annex VIII registration; FRIA-triggering systems are Annex III high-risk and therefore also carry Art 49/Annex VIII registration (non-public variant for law-enforcement/migration/border); the completed FRIA is notified to the market surveillance authority.
- not_high_risk_documentation_flag: a transparency-only, not-high-risk system still owes the Art 50 disclosures; where FRIA was considered and found not to apply, record why (org type/use case did not match the trigger set).
